Who are the hackers?

A white hat hacker or white hacker who practices what is known as “ethical hacking” is a term applied in the IT world to a person whose values ​​oppose violating other computer systems. A white hat hacker focuses on protecting systems, unlike a black hat hacker who tries to hack them. In other words, a white hat hacker is someone who is authorized to use prohibited means to address computer and network security risks The term white hat in Internet slang refers to an ethical computer hacker or, more correctly, a computer security expert, who specializes in test penetration and penetration science in a systematic way to ensure the security of the information systems of any organization, organization or company. Ethical Hacking is a term coined by IBM that means more than just a penetration test. White hat hackers may work in groups. These groups may be called red teams or tiger teams.

One of the first ethical breaches used was to assess the security of the Multics operating system (the real kernel of UNIX and GNU/Linux), by the United States Air Force, for its potential use as a top-secret operating system.
